# POUR Framework — Knowledge Base
# 7 Points of Urgent Resolution for Demo Evaluation

## What POUR Is
POUR flags are critical issues identified during a demo session that require immediate attention and may impact conversion, teacher quality, or student experience. Every demo must be evaluated against all 7 categories.

---

## Category 1: VIDEO
**What it covers**: Recording unavailable, camera off by tutor or student, video quality issues affecting session evaluation or student experience.

**Examples**:
- Tutor's camera is off throughout the demo
- Demo recording not saved or unavailable in LMS
- Video quality so poor that session cannot be evaluated
- Student camera off (impacts interactivity assessment)

**Severity guide**:
- High: Recording completely unavailable — analyst cannot evaluate
- Medium: Camera off but recording exists with audio
- Low: Minor video lag that did not impact session

**Delegation**: Tech (if platform issue), Admin (if recording policy), Counselor (if tutor behavioural)

---

## Category 2: INTERACTION
**What it covers**: Session was one-directional — tutor lectured without student participation, no questions asked or answered, student visibly disengaged.

**Examples**:
- Tutor read from slides/textbook without checking understanding
- Student asked no questions and was not prompted to
- No rapport built between tutor and student
- Session felt like a broadcast, not a tutorial

**Severity guide**:
- High: Student gave very low feedback rating + sales comments confirm lack of engagement
- Medium: Sales agent noted low energy but student feedback was neutral
- Low: Minor interaction gap in one portion of session

**Delegation**: Counselor (for tutor coaching), Product Head (if pattern)

---

## Category 3: TECHNICAL
**What it covers**: Audio issues, platform connectivity, whiteboard malfunction, background noise, session interruptions due to technology.

**Examples**:
- Tutor's audio was unclear or had consistent drop-outs
- Google Meet / Tuitional platform crashed mid-session
- Whiteboard failed to load or share
- Background noise from tutor's environment
- Student could not hear tutor due to microphone issues

**Severity guide**:
- High: Session terminated early due to technical issue
- Medium: Issue persisted for more than 10 minutes
- Low: Brief issue, resolved, did not materially impact session

**Delegation**: Tech (platform issues), Admin (tutor equipment)

---

## Category 4: CANCELLATION
**What it covers**: Demo was cancelled or rescheduled, with or without notice.

**Examples**:
- Tutor cancelled within 24 hours of demo
- Parent cancelled at last minute
- Demo rescheduled multiple times
- Student did not respond to booking confirmation

**Severity guide**:
- High: Cancelled on the day, parent had high intent, opportunity lost
- Medium: Rescheduled once with reasonable notice
- Low: Cancelled with sufficient notice, rebooked successfully

**Delegation**: Admin (tutor-initiated), Sales Agent (parent-initiated), Counselor (if pattern)

---

## Category 5: RESOURCES
**What it covers**: Wrong materials used in demo — incorrect exam board, wrong curriculum level, no past paper when needed, tutor unprepared.

**Examples**:
- Tutor used Cambridge materials for an Edexcel student
- Teacher did not have the student's past paper or set text
- Demo topic was too easy/difficult for the student's actual level
- Tutor used generic notes instead of specification-aligned content
- Wrong syllabus code followed (e.g. taught AS content to IGCSE student)

**Severity guide**:
- High: Entirely wrong curriculum — student confused, likely not to convert
- Medium: Mostly correct but key resource missing (e.g. no past paper)
- Low: Minor resource gap that tutor compensated for

**Delegation**: Product Head (allocation decision), Counselor (curation guidance to tutor)

**Most common pattern**: Resources POUR is overwhelmingly a product/allocation issue. The tutor should be given the correct resources during demo briefing.

---

## Category 6: TIME
**What it covers**: Session was too short, too long, or time was wasted on setup/irrelevant content.

**Examples**:
- Demo lasted only 20 minutes when 45 minutes was agreed
- First 15 minutes wasted setting up whiteboard
- Tutor rushed through topic — pace too fast
- Session ran significantly over time
- Correct topic covered for only 15 of 45 minutes (rest was off-topic)

**Severity guide**:
- High: Session less than 50% of agreed duration with substantive content missed
- Medium: Notable time waste but key points were covered
- Low: Slight pace issue that student mentioned but did not prevent learning

**Delegation**: Admin (scheduling), Counselor (tutor preparation), Product Head (pattern)

---

## Category 7: NO SHOW
**What it covers**: Student, parent, or tutor did not attend the scheduled demo.

**Examples**:
- Student did not join at the agreed time
- Tutor did not show up
- Parent who was required for the demo did not attend
- Demo ran but key decision maker (parent) was absent

**Severity guide**:
- High: Complete no-show, no communication, opportunity lost
- Medium: Partial attendance (student joined, parent did not)
- Low: Joined late with brief window still productive

**Delegation**: Sales Agent (parent/student no-show), Admin (tutor no-show), Counselor (re-scheduling)

---

## POUR Summary Statistics (from historical data)
Most common POUR categories (based on Nov 2025 – Apr 2026 data):
1. **Resources** — Most frequent. Wrong teacher/materials for the student's curriculum.
2. **Interaction** — Second most frequent. One-sided sessions.
3. **Technical** — Third most frequent. Audio and platform issues.
4. **Time** — Fourth. Pace issues and short sessions.
5. **Video** — Fifth. Camera off, recordings unavailable.
6. **Cancellation** — Sixth. Rescheduling patterns.
7. **No Show** — Least frequent. Complete absences.
